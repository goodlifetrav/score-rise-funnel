HIPAA No Media Dispute System

Score Rise Kit | Professional Edition

The "No Media" Logic

Credit bureaus are prohibited from reporting medical data without a physical, signed "Release of Information" (the media) that explicitly authorizes the disclosure of your private health records to third-party financial entities. If the bureau or collection agency cannot produce the original document containing your wet-ink signature, they have no legal standing to report the item and must delete it immediately.

Letter Template

Date:
[Current Date]

To: [Credit Bureau Name]

[Bureau Address]

[City, State, Zip]

RE: Formal Dispute & Request for Media Audit

To Whom It May Concern,

I am writing to formally dispute the following medical account listed on my credit report:

[Medical Provider Name]
[Account Number]
[Collection Agency]

I am requesting a full audit of the media associated with this account. If you cannot provide the original signed contract that authorizes the release of my private medical information, this item must be deleted immediately under HIPAA privacy standards.

Under the HIPAA Privacy Rule, medical providers and their business associates are restricted from sharing protected health information without express written consent. If "the media" for this debt does not exist in your records, its continued reporting constitutes a violation of my federal privacy rights.

Please provide proof of the aforementioned authorization within 30 days or remove the entry from my credit file.

Sincerely,

[Your Legal Name]

[Your Social Security Number]

[Your Address]

Important Notes

  • • Ensure you include a copy of your Driver’s License and a Utility Bill to verify your identity.
  • • Send this letter via Certified Mail with Return Receipt Requested to track delivery.
  • • The bureaus have 30 days to respond. If they fail to provide the media, the law is on your side.
Execution Step CRITICAL: Send this letter only to the Credit Bureaus, NOT the hospital.
